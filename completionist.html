The Achievement Ladder
1
📜 Quest Cape
Complete every quest in the game (270 quests as of early 2026)
The first major milestone. Unlocks the Quest Cape and the title [Name] the Quester. Most of the best gear, areas, and story content is gated behind quests. Do quests first — grind later.
2
🎓 Max Cape
Level 99+ in all 29 skills (120 in elite skills)
The iconic cape of achievement. Grants access to the Max Guild and lets you combine any two skill cape perks into a single Max Cape. Signals you're a serious long-term player. Aim for this before Completionist.
3
🏆 Completionist Cape
Complete all 100 Completionist achievements
Goes far beyond maxing — requires minigame completions, boss kills, area task sets, and much more. The cape itself combines the perks of any three skill capes and signals a truly dedicated player.
4
💎 Trimmed Completionist Cape
All achievements in the game including grace-period tasks
The most prestigious non-boss-related item in RS3. Requires sustained effort across every piece of content including seasonal and time-limited tasks. The trimmed cape glows and is a rare sight in-game.
5
👑 Master Completionist Cape
Trimmed Completionist + Master Quest Cape requirements
The absolute pinnacle of RS3 achievement. Combines Trimmed Comp with the Master Quest Cape (all miniquests, lore diaries, and additional completions). Very few players hold this at any given time. A multi-year journey.
⏱️ Time Estimates: Quest Cape: 200–400 hours. Max Cape: 2,000–5,000 hours depending on methods. Comp Cape: adds 500–1,000 more hours. Trimmed Comp: another 500–2,000 hours. Master Comp: multi-year commitment for most players.

💡 Efficient Max Cape Route
  • Get all combat skills to 99/120 via Slayer — covers Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, Necromancy, Constitution, and Slayer simultaneously
  • Prayer: Gilded altar in PoH. Dragon bones → Frost Dragon bones as budget allows
  • Herblore: Herb runs + buy herbs on GE. Save DXP for this.
  • Summoning: Bulk-make pouches from accumulated charms. War Tortoise → Steel Titan path.
  • Farming: Run patches daily — the skill levels itself passively if you do runs consistently
  • Fishing + Cooking: Fish rocktails at Living Rock Caverns. Cook all of them.
  • Smithing: Gold ore + Goldsmith Gauntlets. One of the fastest artisan skills.
  • Agility: Hefin course at 77+. Put on a podcast and click.
  • Dungeoneering: Rushed floors in groups at Daemonheim. 5-person teams significantly faster.
  • Invention: Augment and level gear during all training. XP from using augmented equipment.

🏆 Completionist Cape — All 100 Comp Achievements

Requirements: Max Cape + Quest Cape + 100 specific Completionist achievements covering minigames, bosses, area tasks, and more. Check the Achievements tab in-game under the "Completionist" category for your exact remaining tasks.
Major Requirement Categories
📜 Quest & Story Requirements
  • Quest Cape — all main quests completed
  • Master Quest Cape requirements (miniquests)
  • Lore journals and diaries completed
  • Fort Forinthry storyline complete
  • All God Wars questlines finished
⚔️ Boss & PvM Requirements
  • Final Boss achievement — kill every boss at least once
  • GWD1 bosses: Bandos, Saradomin, Zamorak, Armadyl kills
  • GWD2 bosses: Vindicta, Helwyr, Twin Furies, Gregorovic
  • Nex (solo or team) — at least one kill
  • Araxxor and Araxxi — at least one kill each
  • TzKal-Zuk — Igneous Kal obtained (Fight Kiln completion)
  • Rasial — Necromancy capstone boss
🗺️ Area Achievement Sets
  • Lumbridge & Draynor — Beginner through Hard
  • Varrock — Easy through Hard
  • Falador — Easy through Hard
  • Ardougne — Easy through Hard
  • Morytania — Easy through Hard
  • Prifddinas — all tiers (requires Song of the Elves)
  • Desert, Wilderness, Seers Village, etc.
🎮 Minigame Requirements
  • Barbarian Assault — Queen Black Dragon kill / role completions
  • Pest Control — points earned at each rank
  • Fight Caves / Fight Kiln — completion for cape
  • Dominion Tower — floor completion milestones
  • Soul Wars — points requirements
  • Stealing Creation — some completions
  • Player-Owned Ports — Ship voyages and region unlocks
💡 Efficient Comp Strategy: Work on Comp achievements alongside your normal gameplay. Do minigames in downtime between Slayer tasks. Complete area tasks in batches when you're already in a region for quests. Don't grind Comp achievements in isolation — integrate them into your existing routine.

💎 Trimmed Completionist Cape

The Hardest Non-Boss Achievement: Trimmed Comp requires completing every achievement in the game, including time-gated and seasonal content. It demands consistent long-term effort across all areas of the game.
Additional Requirements Beyond Comp Cape
⏳ Time-Gated Content
  • Seasonal quests that only appear during holiday events
  • Grace period achievements — tasks that have a deadline to complete
  • Monthly D&D completions (Giant Oyster, Balthazar's, etc.)
  • Penguin Hide & Seek — consistent weekly participation
  • Warbands — daily attendance over extended period
🎮 Deep Minigame Completions
  • Barbarian Assault — all role completions at high level
  • Soul Wars — extensive point accumulation
  • Dominion Tower — all floors and challenges
  • Conquest — PvP minigame points requirement
  • Fist of Guthix — points for cosmetics
  • TzHaar Fight Pit — participate and win
🏆 Endgame Boss Completions
  • Insane Final Boss — kill every boss 100+ times
  • Boss collection log completion on key bosses
  • Hard mode versions of specific bosses
  • Raids participation and completions (when added)
  • Elite Dungeons — all 3 completed
📊 Skill Milestones
  • 200M XP in certain skills for specific achievements
  • Completionist-specific skill challenges
  • All skill pets obtained (very rare drops from training)
  • Player-Owned Farm — breed all animals with Trait Ravensworn
  • Archaeology — all artefacts collected

👑 Master Completionist Cape

The Absolute Pinnacle: Combines Trimmed Completionist with all Master Quest Cape requirements. Fewer than 0.1% of players hold this at any given time. This is a multi-year goal even for dedicated players.
📋 Master Quest Cape Requirements (on top of Quest Cape)
  • Complete all miniquests — shorter side quests not on the main quest list
  • Complete all lore diaries — books and journals found throughout the world
  • Complete all quest-adjacent tasks — specific post-quest activities
  • Ratcatcher's training and other skill-related post-quest content
  • All post-quest rewards unlocked and collected

🗺️ Treasure Trail Collection Log

Get the Treasure Trail Collection Log book from Zaida near the Grand Exchange entrance. It tracks every unique reward from clue scrolls. Complete clues whenever they drop — never discard one.

Easy Clues
Scan tasks in Gielinor. Cosmetic hatwear, trimmed armour, and novelty rewards. Do these first to get comfortable with clue mechanics.
Medium Clues
Emote + scan tasks. Some valuable items including god pages and unique cosmetics. Good for mid-game log hunting.
Hard Clues
Multi-step with combat requirements. God armour trimming, statuettes worth 1M+. Most popular tier for casual farming.
Elite Clues
Long multi-step clues. Third-age armour (extremely rare and valuable). Elite clue rewards are the most sought-after in Treasure Trails.
Master Clues
The hardest tier. Combines elements from all other clue types. Completionist necessity — unique rewards only available here.
Sandy Clues
Summer beach seasonal clues. Exclusive cosmetics only available during the Beach event. Check in-game calendar for active dates.
💡 Clue Strategy: Always do clues when they drop during Slayer or bossing. Stack them if possible. A Clue Scroll box can hold multiple. Focus on doing clues consistently rather than trying to grind them specifically — the drops will accumulate naturally.
